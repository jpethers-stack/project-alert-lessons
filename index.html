Why Is It Hard to Quit Vaping/Smoking?

Most teens — and most adults — don't use nicotine. But for someone who already does, quitting is tough. Why? Click each reason.

🧠 What is addiction? (click to reveal)
Ask a student to define it first — then reveal.
Addiction is when someone's brain and body start to really need something, even if it's not good for them. Imagine suddenly not being able to use your phone at all — restless, can't relax or focus. Nicotine addiction feels like that, but stronger, and it affects the body as well as the mind. The good news: people CAN quit — the cravings and jittery feelings fade, and organs (especially the lungs) can recover. The sooner someone quits, the less life-threatening damage.

Myth vs. Fact — flip each card

Vaping/smoking calms you down & relieves stress.
flip for the fact
It only seems calming: addicted people feel irritable in withdrawal. Nicotine just relieves the withdrawal it caused — until the level drops and the bad feelings return.
Vaping/smoking keeps you thin.
flip for the fact
It numbs taste and suppresses appetite, but doesn't change eating habits. It is not an effective way to manage weight — and it carries serious health harms.
E-cigarettes / vapes are safer than regular cigarettes.
flip for the fact
Not proven. Many who switch end up using both — taking in even more nicotine and harmful chemicals.

1What gets people to quit?

  • Feeling sick
  • Losing good friends

  • Don't like a substance controlling their life
  • Friends want them to stop
2How do people quit?
click for typical answers
  • Cold turkey
  • Parents/friends help them
  • Friends quit together
  • Treatment programs
  • Do other things at the urge — hobby, exercise, eat, talk to friends, breathe deeply
  • Reduce use, then stop · try several times
3What are the good things you get from quitting?
click for typical answers
  • Better health & better at sports
  • Whiter teeth · don't smell
  • Don't lose friends
  • Not nervous · feel more in control
  • Happy family · save money
4How can you help someone trying to quit?
click for typical answers
  • Moral support, encouragement, showing you care
  • Look forward to things you'll do together when they quit
  • Tell them the harms of smoking & the benefits of quitting
  • Tell them about treatment programs
  • Say you love them and you're worried for them

It Takes Courage — and It Can Be Done

Addiction to nicotine is powerful, and quitting isn't easy — but the benefits are real:

🫨
Healthier lungs
💨
Breathe easier
💓
Less organ damage
💰
More money
✅
No cravings
💪
More in control
Remember: Most students your age don't use nicotine — you're with the majority. And whether it's quitting nicotine or changing any habit, it takes courage — but if you really want to, you can do it.
Supporting someone is powerful too. Moral support, encouragement, and showing you care can help a person you love quit. If a feeling ever gets too heavy to handle alone, talk to a trusted adult or call/text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) anytime.
Exit Ticket: Write down ONE way you could support someone who wants to stop using.
